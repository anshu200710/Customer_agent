JCB AI Voice Call System — What I Built & What I Want

What This System Does
I built an AI-powered voice call system for Rajesh Motors JCB service in Rajasthan. When a JCB machine breaks down, the customer calls our service number. Instead of a human agent picking up, my AI agent "Priya" answers the call, talks to the customer in natural Hindi and Rajasthani dialect, collects all the complaint details, and automatically registers the service complaint in our system — without any human involvement.
The entire call takes 45 to 90 seconds. The engineer gets notified immediately.

Why I Built This
Before this system, customers had to call our service center and wait for a human agent to pick up. Agents were busy, calls were missed, complaint details were entered manually and sometimes incorrectly. Customers in rural Rajasthan speak Rajasthani dialect and broken Hindi — not everyone understood them properly.
I wanted a system that:

Answers every call instantly, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week
Understands Rajasthani dialect — words like "tel nikal ryo", "dhak gayi", "band padi", "race nahi lag rahi"
Collects complaint data accurately without human errors
Registers the complaint automatically in our backend system
Feels like talking to a real human agent, not a robot


How It Works — Step by Step
Step 1 — Call Comes In
Customer calls the JCB service number. Twilio receives the call and connects it to my Node.js server.
Step 2 — AI Greets
AI agent Priya says: "Ji, machine number bataiye?"
If the machine number was already sent in the call URL (from our app), Priya greets the customer by name and skips straight to asking about the problem.
Step 3 — Conversation
Priya asks questions one by one, in this order:

Machine number (4–8 digits)
What problem (complaint type)
Machine status (running or completely stopped)
City / location
Mobile number

If the customer gives multiple answers in one sentence, Priya captures all of them at once and skips ahead.
Step 4 — Data Extraction
Every customer reply goes through two layers:

Regex extraction — catches numbers, city names, complaint keywords in both Hindi and Devanagari script (भीलवाड़ा, चालू नहीं, बंद है)
AI (Groq LLaMA 3.3 70B) — understands natural language, Rajasthani dialect, and ambiguous replies

Step 5 — Complaint Submitted
Once all 5 required fields are collected, the system automatically calls our backend API and registers the complaint. Priya tells the customer their complaint number and says the engineer will call soon.
Step 6 — Engineer Gets Job
The complaint appears in our system with all details. The nearest engineer is assigned automatically.

What Technology I Used

Twilio — for receiving phone calls and converting speech to text (STT)
Google Wavenet Voice (hi-IN-Wavenet-D) — natural Hindi female voice for Priya
Groq + LLaMA 3.3 70B — AI brain that understands the conversation
Node.js + Express — backend server that handles the call flow
Rajesh Motors Backend API — where complaints are registered


What Is Working Well Right Now

AI answers in under 1 second after customer speaks
Understands 30+ Rajasthani dialect words and phrases
Correctly extracts machine numbers, phone numbers, city names from natural speech
Handles noise and irrelevant replies — repeats the same question without confusing the customer
Rejects invalid phone numbers (6-digit machine numbers mistaken as phones)
City name fuzzy matching — "Bhilwada" is understood as "Bhilwara", "Jaypur" as "Jaipur"
Devanagari script support — "भीलवाड़ा" and "चालू नहीं" are correctly parsed from STT output
job_location auto-defaults to "Onsite" if customer never mentions it
Pre-identified machine calls skip 2–3 turns and finish faster
Graceful hangup after 3 silences with polite message


What Needs to Be Improved
This is the honest list of what I want fixed and upgraded:
1. STT Accuracy for Rajasthani Dialect
Twilio's speech-to-text sometimes misheard Rajasthani words badly. "Bhilwara" became "Bhilwada", numbers were sometimes completely wrong. I want to test Deepgram STT or Google STT with custom vocabulary hints for JCB-specific words like machine model names, city names, and complaint keywords. Better STT = everything downstream works better.
2. Machine Number Lookup Mid-Call
When a customer gives their machine number, the system should immediately validate it against our database and pull the customer's name, city and registered phone. Right now this lookup exists but is not always fast enough. I want it to run in parallel while Priya is still speaking the next question — no wait time for the customer.
3. Complaint Detail Capture
Right now I capture the complaint type (Engine Not Starting, Hydraulic Failure, etc.) but I am not capturing enough detail. A good human agent would ask follow-up questions like "How long has this problem been there?" or "Did any warning light come on?". I want Priya to ask 1–2 natural follow-up questions when time allows, so the engineer arrives better prepared.
4. Multi-Language Confidence
Some customers speak a mix of Hindi, Rajasthani and English in the same sentence. The AI handles this but occasionally misses a word. I want to add a language detection confidence score so if the AI is not confident about what was said, it asks once to confirm rather than silently proceeding with wrong data.
5. Call Summary SMS
After every successful complaint registration, the customer should receive an SMS with their complaint number and expected response time. Currently only the voice confirmation is given. SMS gives them a reference they can use later.
6. Callback When Line Busy / Missed
If a customer calls and the system fails for any reason (server timeout, API down), right now that call is lost. I want an automatic callback queue — if complaint registration fails, the system should retry calling the customer within 10 minutes and complete the complaint.
7. Live Dashboard
I want a real-time dashboard showing today's calls, complaint status, which calls succeeded, which failed, average call duration, most common complaint types by city. Right now all this lives in server logs only.
8. Voice Tone Improvement
Priya's voice is Google Wavenet which sounds good but still slightly robotic on some phrases. I want to test ElevenLabs Hindi voice or fine-tune the SSML prosody (speed, pause, pitch) so the call feels even more human. Based on my actual recordings of real human agents, the ideal is short bursts of 3–6 words, warm tone, occasional "achha" and "haan" acknowledgments.
9. Engineer Confirmation Loop
After the complaint is registered, if the assigned engineer has not called the customer within 30 minutes, the AI should automatically call the customer again and either connect them to the engineer directly or escalate to a human supervisor.
10. Analytics on Missed Data
When a call ends without collecting all required fields (customer hung up, 3 silences, turn limit hit), I want to log exactly which field was missing and what the last thing the customer said was. This helps me understand what conversation patterns need to be improved.

My Vision for This System
Right now this handles basic complaint registration. My vision is to expand this into a complete JCB service lifecycle voice assistant:

Customer calls → complaint registered (done)
Engineer calls back → AI assists engineer with job details
Job completed → AI calls customer to confirm satisfaction
Warranty / AMC queries → AI answers from customer history
Parts availability → AI checks stock and gives ETA

Every JCB customer in Rajasthan should feel like they have a dedicated, always-available service coordinator in their pocket who speaks their language, knows their machine, and gets things done in under 2 minutes.
